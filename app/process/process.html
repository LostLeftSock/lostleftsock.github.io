My Design Process

The best experiences come from understanding the user’s needs now and anticipating their needs in the future.

Above all, be adaptable

Each project brings with it unique challenges to solve and it’s more important to look at the big picture, and what needs to be accomplished overall, than follow a rigid process. I’ve joined projects at all different stages, and I’ve adjusted my approach each time to deliver what will make the biggest impact.

1. Define the strategy

Good requirements help create a solid foundation. This phase starts with defining the overall vision and goals, brand strategy, feature priorities for both users and business, and establishing the KPIs and success criteria.

2. Research & empathize

We take time to get to know your users. We’ll start this phase with data and competitive analysis, user testing, interviews and surveys. We’ll end with creating personas, user flows, and the Information architecture needed for the product.

4. Design for people

Now it’s time to design for the people using the product. This phase includes the visual design, typography, copywriting, art, and interactions all working together to delight those using it.

3. Ideate concepts

Solving big problems takes new and creative solutions. We combine the research from the last phase with mood boards, wireframes, prototypes and more, which allows for the discovery of real solutions to the user’s needs.

5. Measure successes & failures

A launch of a product isn’t the end, it’s only the beginning. It’s important to understand what worked, and what didn’t, now that it's being used in the real world. Time to A/B Test, gather more data, and watch how things are doing.

6. Improve with iteration

Now with new data in hand, it’s time to take the outcome of the last phases and make focused and deliberate adjustments to further refine the experience. Even small changes can have a big impact.
